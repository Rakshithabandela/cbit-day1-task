Jeon Jung-kook (Korean: ; born September 1, 1997) is a South Korean singer who goes by the stage name Jungkook. He is a vocalist and band member of the South Korean boy group BTS. Three solo songs by Jungkook have been included in the BTS discography: "Begin" in 2016, "Euphoria" in 2018, and "My Time" in 2020. All three songs reached the top 10 on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart. Additionally, he performed "Stay Alive" on the soundtrack for the BTS-based webcomic 7Fates: Chakho. He appeared on the Charlie Puth song "Left and Right" from 2022, which peaked at number 22 on the U.S. singles chart.The Billboard Hot 100. He released "Dreamers" later that year, making him the first South Korean artist to have a song on the official FIFA World Cup soundtrack. He later sang it at the 2022 FIFA World Cup Opening Ceremony. The solo songs by Jungkook have also done well on streaming services, with "Dreamers," "Stay Alive," and "Left and Right" all breaking the record for a Korean soloist's highest debut single on the Spotify Global list.

Jeon Jung-kook, known professionally as Jung Kook, is the youngest member of a South Korean K-pop idol boy group, BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan/Bulletproof Boy Scouts/Beyond the Scene), who were formed by Big Hit Entertainment. His roles in the group are main vocalist, lead dancer, and supporting rapper. He debuted with the rest of the members of BTS as a hip-hop idol group on the June 13, 2013, with the song "No More Dream", later producing songs in a different genres. At 14, he was sent to Los Angeles to learn different styles of dance, because a producer said he had no emotion in his dancing.
